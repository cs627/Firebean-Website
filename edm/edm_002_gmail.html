SECTION 03 — CASE STUDY
GOVERNMENT Constitutional and Mainland Affairs Bureau
 
GOVERNMENT & PUBLIC SECTOR

Beyond Information: How HK's Best Campaigns Turn Policy into Experience

Revitalizing Civic Engagement: The “Elections in the Community” Roving Exhibition

Transforming complex electoral processes into engaging, accessible content for all demographics.

Facing public apathy towards the 2026 Legislative Council election, the Constitutional and Mainland Affairs Bureau sought to demystify the electoral process. Firebean designed the “Elections in the Community” Roving Interactive Exhibition, transforming complex civic information into interactive, family-friendly games across major malls.

This initiative moved beyond conventional outreach, successfully engaging diverse demographics, particularly younger professionals. By gamifying learning and providing direct simulations, we ensured the electoral process was not only understood but actively embraced, demonstrating that serious civic messages can be effectively communicated through innovative experiential design. We transformed a standard civic education campaign into a high-footfall destination, generating significant organic social media amplification and measurable public interaction.

Key Project Deliverables:

→Turnkey execution of a multi-district roving exhibition, ensuring broad public access and engagement.
→Integration of physical structures (3D-printed sculptures) with interactive digital panels for trackable engagement.
→Measurable increase in public understanding and positive sentiment towards civic participation.
